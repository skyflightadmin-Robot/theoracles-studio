# Modern Minimalist Website Inspiration

Curated for **theoracles.studio** — we're looking for sites that keep the same restraint (few links, lots of whitespace, text-forward, no visual noise) but feel more polished, modern, and professional.

Your current site does a lot right: one headline, a short intro, four text links, a social footer. The sites below push that same restraint further with better typography, more considered spacing, and subtle craft details (micro-animations, confident hero photography, thoughtful type pairings).

---

## 1. Design galleries — browse curated examples

These are the best places to flip through hundreds of sites in the "quiet, polished" category. Each is hand-curated, so the signal-to-noise ratio is high.

- **[Minimal Gallery](https://minimal.gallery/)** — Hand-picked daily, laser-focused on minimalist sites with generous whitespace and restrained palettes. This is probably the single best match for your aesthetic direction.
- **[SiteInspire](https://www.siteinspire.com/websites/categories/minimal)** — Minimal category filter. Has been curating since 2010 and stays consistently high-quality. Filter by "typography" or "portfolio" for more targeted browsing.
- **[Land-book](https://land-book.com/)** — Updated daily; useful for landing-page thinking (one strong headline, clean nav).
- **[Awwwards — Minimal](https://www.awwwards.com/websites/minimal/)** — Industry award site; the "minimal" tag collection is a goldmine.
- **[Godly](https://godly.website/)** — Curated "divinely" inspired web design; many faith-adjacent and contemplative aesthetics here that might resonate with your mission.

## 3. Mission-driven / nonprofit / storytelling orgs — closest to your purpose

These are nonprofits and purpose-led sites that manage to stay visually restrained while communicating mission clearly. Best reference points for tone and information architecture.

- **[Charity: Water](https://www.charitywater.org/)** — Often cited as the gold standard for nonprofit web design: strong hero, minimal chrome, photography carries the emotional weight, donate CTA is present but never shouty.
- **[Gates Foundation](https://www.gatesfoundation.org/)** — Clean header, ample whitespace, black-and-white restraint, but still communicates scale and seriousness. Good reference for an "About Us" heavy site.
- **[Malala Fund](https://malala.org/)** — Personal-story-first homepage, clean typography, warmth without being cluttered.
- **[Kindness.org](https://kindness.org/)** — Story-driven, clean layout, good example of inviting user contribution without being pushy.
- **[The Marshall Project](https://www.themarshallproject.org/)** — Journalism nonprofit; clean editorial typography, minimal ornamentation, every piece of content has room to breathe.
- **[Pro Publica](https://www.propublica.org/)** — Similar editorial-minimal approach; mission + portfolio-of-work mix feels relevant to you.

## How to use this list

When you review these with Dabe, it's useful to capture **three things per site** that resonate:

1. **Layout** — how is the homepage structured? Single-screen? Scroll-driven? What does the header look like?
2. **Type** — is it serif-led, sans-led, or a mix? How big is the hero text? How much hierarchy?
3. **Tone** — does it feel warm, austere, confident, playful? Which of those words should theoracles.studio feel like?

Once you have a shortlist of 3–5 favorites, they become the design DNA targets for the redesign and we can start translating those moves into what Premium/WordPress can deliver.
